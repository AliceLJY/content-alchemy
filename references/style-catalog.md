## Style Combination Rules

> Styles can be combined in pairs for unique effects. Format: `{Style A prompt suffix}, combined with {Style B prompt suffix}`

### Recommended Combinations

| Combo | Effect | Best For |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Cyberpunk + Ukiyo-e | Cyber-Japanese fusion, sci-fi meets tradition | AI + Eastern culture |
| Van Gogh + Cyberpunk | Swirling starry night meets neon city | tech vs humanity |
| Claymation + Cyberpunk | Cute clay figures in cyber world | lighthearted tech topics |
| Felt Craft + Ink Brush | Warm texture + Eastern mood | healing Chinese style |
| Botticelli + Kusama | Classical grace meets polka dots | dialogue across eras |
| Dunhuang + Cyberpunk | Ancient murals meet neon future | tradition vs technology |
| Hopper + Munch | Urban loneliness × existential anxiety | modern spiritual crisis |
| Matisse + Feng Zikai | East-West innocence and simplicity | philosophy of life |
| Warhol + New Year Print | Pop consumerism meets folk festivity | Chinese consumer culture |
| Banksy + Basquiat | Double street art energy | social critique |

### Combination Principles

- **Max 2 styles** per combination (3+ becomes muddled)
- **High contrast** combos create more visual impact
- **Same-tone** combos tend to be flat; cross-tone is better
- **East + West** combos often produce the most striking results

## Auto-Rotation Logic

When used with automated pipelines:

1. Analyze article tone/mood
2. Select 4-5 matching candidate styles (single or combo)
3. Exclude styles used in the last 5 articles
4. Pick one; announce selection to user (user can override)
5. Log selection to history file
